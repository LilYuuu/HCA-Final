Alms Giving Food to the Starving Ghost

Lin Tinggui. Southern Song. Museum of Fine Arts Boston.

Giving food to hungry ghosts is the Mengshan Alms Giving in Buddhism. Mengshan is a mountain in Ya'an, Sichuan Province. Legend says that in the past there was a foreign monk named Master Ganlu who once stayed in this mountain. In accordance with the Yankou ritual rules (焰口仪规), he made a Mengshan Amls Giving (蒙山施食仪文) to save the ghosts.

There are two types of Mengshan Alms. Smaller mengshan is held in the temple evening class, while Larger mengshan must be hosted by a monk. The content of the chanting is similar to that of Smaller mengshan, but the number of mantras is more than that of Smaller mengshan. The mage speaks to the six realms of reincarnation (heaven, man, asura, hell, hungry ghost, and animal) group spirits, so that their sin karma is eliminated, and the way of goodness is promoted.
