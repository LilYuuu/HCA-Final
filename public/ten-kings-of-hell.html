The emergence of "Ten Yamas of Hell" has led people to believe that everyone will be judged by Yama after death, regardless of whether they were rich or poor. This undoubtedly provides a sense of relief for the common people who are in a hierarchical society.

The Dun Huang "Ten Kings of Hell" coexisted in 5 volumes: 3 columns in France and 2 columns in Britain. The painting has no words and descriptions. Perhaps the story of "Ten Kings of Hell" was widely known to the people at that time, and the painting alone is enough to make people understand it. This may also be the world's earliest painted comic strip, which is very precious.




Ten Kings of Hell

Lu Xinzhong, Southern Song, Eigen-ji Temple, Japan.

Ten Kings of Hell depicts the ten Yamas of the underworld. These paintings mainly depict the punishments towards the evil people after their death in the hell, with a strong admonition. The story of ten Yamas is recorded in ancient Buddhist classics.

During Xiaozong, Guangzong, and Ningzong periods in Southern Song Dynasty, the North-South confrontation was relatively stable and the society was slightly prosperous. Painting developed tremendously during this period. In addition to court painting academies, there were also a considerable number of folk painter groups. In addition to mural paintings, hanging scroll paintings were popular to meet the needs of temples and the public for making merit.

During the same period, the communication between Japan and China became more frequent. A large number of monks and trade missions came to China, which also stimulated the prosperity of hanging scroll paintings. The foreign trade mission brought Chinese paintings back to Japan. At that time, Mingzhou Port (now Ningbo) was the center of foreign trade and cultural exports to Japan. It can be seen that foreign trade demand stimulated the development of folk Buddhist paintings.
