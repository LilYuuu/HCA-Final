Gao Qipei, Qing. Shanghai Museum.

Gao Qipei's painting style was influenced by Shen Zhou, Chen Chun, Bada Shanren, and Fu Shan, which in turn influenced the "Eight Eccentrics of Yangzhou". When he paints, he does not only use his fingers, but any part of his hand to paint with nails, palms and back of hands, according to the thickness of lines. His paintings are full of ecstasy and freshness.

Finger painting originated in the Tang Dynasty, and Gao Qipei pushed it to a peak in the Qing Dynasty. Anecdote says that Gao Qipei started to learn painting at the age of eight, and he was still unable to form his own style when he was 20. There was a geyser in the room, so he dipped his finger in the water. After waking up, thinking of this dream, he started to paint with his fingers, which made him even more comfortable. If this dream is true, it is nothing more than what the painter thinks. It is a slumped emotion in the heart. Relieving through the dream is also one of the results of the painter's persistent understanding about painting.
